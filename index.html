The Stube Legacy

Nearly Six Decades of History

Since 1966, the Bier Stube stood as more than just a bar—it was a rite of passage for Ohio State students, a sanctuary for locals, and a time capsule of authentic Columbus culture. From its humble beginnings as a plumbing store to becoming the most beloved dive bar in the University District, every inch of worn wood and every faded photograph told a story.

Under Craig Kempton's stewardship since 1996—first as a doorman, then as owner from 2004—the Stube maintained its authentic character. "Keep it cheap, keep it cool" wasn't just a motto; it was a promise to the community. Where else could you find 60-ounce pitchers for $5 and know every dollar saved meant another memory made?

When we closed our doors at 1497 N. High Street in September 2024, it wasn't goodbye—it was "see you later." Every piece of the original bar, from the legendary jukebox to the weathered bar stools, has been preserved, waiting for our triumphant return.
